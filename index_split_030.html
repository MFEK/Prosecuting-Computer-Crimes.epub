This theory has not been applied in a criminal case. In civil cases, the plaintiff must prove damage under factors listed in section 1030(c)(4)(A)(i)(I)-(V). See page 42 for a list of these factors. Civil plaintiffs do not have section 1030(a)(2) available to them. Therefore, the flexibility courts have shown toward the definition of damage in civil cases may not apply to criminal cases. Further, the trade-secret aspect of Shurgard may limit its applicability. Prosecutors should apply this theory with caution in any criminal prosecution.
